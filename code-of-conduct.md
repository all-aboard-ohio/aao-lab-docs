# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers of AAO Data Lab pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

**Behavior that contributes to a positive environment:**

- Being kind and respectful in all interactions
- Welcoming contributors of all experience levels without condescension
- Giving and receiving constructive feedback graciously
- Crediting others' work, ideas, and research
- Prioritizing the community's long-term health over short-term wins
- Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them

**Unacceptable behavior:**

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Dismissive or condescending comments about a contributor's skill level
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Personal attacks or sustained off-topic political arguments
- Any conduct a reasonable person would consider inappropriate for a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all AAO Data Lab spaces, including GitHub repositories, Slack channels, video calls, and any in-person events associated with the project.

---

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by:
- Sending a direct message to a project maintainer on Slack
- Emailing the All Aboard Ohio organization directly

All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled confidentially. Reporters will not face retaliation.

Consequences for violations may range from a private warning to permanent removal from the community, depending on the severity and pattern of behavior.
