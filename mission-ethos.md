# Mission & Ethos

## What We Are Building

AAO Data Lab is the open-source software and data arm of All Aboard Ohio. We build tools that support the case for investing in passenger rail and connected public transit — tools designed to be persuasive, accurate, and accessible to everyone from a policy staffer to a commuter scrolling their phone.

We are not a think tank or a lobbying firm. We are builders. We turn publicly available data into interactive visualizations, economic models, and dashboards that help people understand what is at stake and what is possible.

---

## Why Passenger Rail

Passenger rail is fundamentally about access — to jobs, to education, to family, to opportunity. It is one of the most effective ways to increase economic mobility, reduce the cost of living for working households, and connect mid-sized cities that have been cut off from each other for decades.

Ohio is one of the most densely populated states in the country with one of the least developed intercity rail networks. Cities that should be 90-minute regional neighbors — Columbus, Cleveland, Cincinnati, Toledo, Dayton — require two-hour drives. This is not an accident. It reflects decades of underinvestment in the shared infrastructure that underpins how a region actually functions.

The tools we build help make that case with rigor and clarity: ridership projections, economic multipliers, job access analyses, and comparisons to peer states and countries that have invested in the network Ohio has not yet built.

---

## Rail Needs the Whole System

Passenger rail does not work in isolation. A train that drops you at a station with no way to get to your destination is not a complete trip.

This means AAO Data Lab's scope includes the full picture of connected mobility:

- **Local transit** (bus, BRT, light rail) that completes the first and last mile
- **Bike and pedestrian infrastructure** connecting stations to neighborhoods
- **Station area planning** — the walkability, density, and mixed-use development that makes a station a destination, not just a stop
- **Regional coordination** between transit agencies, municipalities, and intercity services

We care about rail because it is the spine. But the spine only works with the rest of the body.

---

## Beyond Ohio

Ohio is where we start. But the problems we are solving — underfunded intercity rail, fragmented regional transit, communities cut off from opportunity — are not unique to Ohio.

The tools we build are designed to be reusable:

- Data pipelines that ingest publicly available GTFS, census, and BTS feeds can be parameterized for any state
- Economic models that work for Ohio work for Indiana, Michigan, Pennsylvania, or any corridor
- Visualization components are generic by design — swap the data, get a new analysis

We document everything so that advocates in other states can fork our tools, substitute their data, and make their own case. A win for intercity rail in any state is a proof point for every state.

---

## What We Focus On

**Economic impact and quality of life.** Our work centers on what rail investment means for people's daily lives — commute times, housing costs, access to employment, regional GDP, property values near stations, and the fiscal health of the communities that a rail network connects.

**Visualization-first.** The most rigorous analysis means nothing if it cannot be communicated. We invest in making data beautiful, legible, and shareable. A chart that fits in a tweet can move a policy conversation. An interactive map can change someone's mind.

**Rigor above speed.** We would rather publish one carefully sourced model than ten speculative ones. We document data sources, note methodology assumptions, and acknowledge uncertainty. Our credibility is the most valuable thing we have.

---

## What We Do Not Do

- We do not take positions on specific legislation. We build tools that inform decisions — we do not make them.
- We do not speculate with data. All outputs are grounded in cited, publicly available sources.
- We do not build for engagement or virality at the expense of accuracy.

## What Makes Someone a Great Contributor Here

You do not need to care about trains. But you do need to care about the question underneath the trains:

*What would it look like if the places people live, work, and move between actually worked for them?*

If that question interests you — and if you want to build tools that make the answer clearer — you are in the right place.

## Our Disciplines

Great work at AAO Data Lab requires people with very different skills working together:

| Discipline | What They Bring |
|---|---|
| **Software Developers** | Interactive tools, APIs, data pipelines, maps |
| **Data Analysts & Engineers** | Cleaned datasets, models, reproducible analysis |
| **UI/UX Designers** | Clarity, accessibility, visual communication |
| **Policy Analysts** | Legislative context, source credibility, framing |
| **Project Managers** | Coordination, issue scoping, milestone tracking |
| **Researchers** | Background literature, comparable programs, citations |
| **Communicators** | Writing, documentation, outreach materials |

None of these disciplines is more valuable than another. The map is no good without the data. The data is no good without the model. The model is no good without the story. The story is no good without the design. Good civic technology requires all of it.
